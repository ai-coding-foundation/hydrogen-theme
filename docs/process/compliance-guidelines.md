# Compliance Guidelines

## Purpose

Regulatory guardrails for supplement marketing claims, content creation, and review moderation to ensure FDA and FTC compliance.

---

## FDA Dietary Supplement Disclaimer

The following disclaimer **must** appear on all product pages and marketing materials:

> These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.

---

## Prohibited Claims Checklist

- [ ] Never claim to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease
- [ ] Never reference specific medical conditions as treatable
- [ ] Never use before/after medical claims
- [ ] Never guarantee specific health outcomes

---

## Approved Language Patterns

| Instead of…                | Use…                                |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| "Treats insomnia"          | "Supports healthy sleep patterns"   |
| "Cures brain fog"          | "Supports mental clarity and focus" |
| "Fixes hormonal imbalance" | "Supports hormonal balance"         |
| "Prevents bone loss"       | "Supports bone health"              |

---

## Review Moderation Guidelines

- Flag and remove customer reviews that make disease claims
- Train support team on compliant response language
- Do not incentivize reviews with health outcome language

---

## Content Review Process

All of the following must be reviewed for compliance before publishing:

- [ ] Product descriptions
- [ ] Marketing emails
- [ ] Social posts
- [ ] Research Hub articles
